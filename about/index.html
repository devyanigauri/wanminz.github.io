What Does Climate Change Have Anything to Do with You?

Severe Weather

  1. Sudden and severe weather changes can leave you unprepared for them [1]
  2. Nursing homes are in hurricane-areas and immediate evacuation would be tough [3]
  3. Odd weather patterns and frequent wildfires increases the amount of pollen, dust, and smoke in the air [6]
  4. The elderly tend to fall victim to weather events like hurricanes and floods [7]

Reduced Air Quality

  1. If you are already suffering from heart or lung disease, the air pollution (like smog) will only make it worse [3]
  2. Because of the warming tempatures, ground-level ozones will form and increase seasonal allergies [6]

Spreading of Illnesses

  1. Elderly are more sensitive to environmental changes and exposures to toxins and illnesses [4]
  2. Warmer temperatures will breed more ticks and mosquitos who carry diseases that can be easily spread, especially those with weak immune systems

Extreme Heat

  1. Your central nervous system is sensitive to high internal temperatures causing memory loss, confusion, and delirium. Because it is hot, your heart has to work harder, which increases the risk for myocardial infraction [4]
  2. Senior citizens are the most vulnerable to heat-related illnesses like malaria and malnutrition [2]
  3. The heat exposure can increase the risk of illness particularly those with diabetes, congestive heart failure, or any other chronic health conditions as sensitivity to heat goes up [6]
